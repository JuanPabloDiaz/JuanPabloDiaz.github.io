Lab 3: Color and Interface Design

Lab 3: My Color Scheme

For this lab, you will create a color scheme that you think reflects you. You will use that color scheme to create a palette that will then be used for your one page site about you.
Instructions
Pick a color that you think reflects who you are.
Go to any color scheme creator like http://colorschemedesigner.com/ to create your own color scheme.
Come up with a palette that will consist of your chosen color scheme and shades of those colors. If you use Color Scheme Designer, you are provided with variations of the colors in your scheme.
Turn the page you created for “Setup your web space” into your own personal one page site using your color palette. You can include your profile information, include a picture of yourself, link to any of your network profiles (whatever you're willing to share), link to your assignments or anything that is related to you. If you can, create custom graphics or use stock graphics or imagery but they are not required. If you use stock graphics or imagery please link to the original source. No premade templates allowed.
Validate your page at http://validator.w3.org/
Submit your link (please make sure it is not broken!) and a short paragraph explaining your color choices. (If you use an external stylesheet, please include a link to that as well.)
Tip: Before you start styling, add a list of the colors at the top of your CSS file. You could include the name of the color and what the color is for. I started doing this because it made the styling process go a lot smoother and quicker by having the colors in one place. I used to just grab colors from my Photoshop design mockup as I needed them!


Example:
/*=================================
Colors
=============
#363636:dark grey = body copy
#6c214e:dark pink = headings
#21696e:dark turquoise = links
#ffecf7:light pink = hover/current nav link
==================================*/
